Football Events

  • Each team plays against another in the group stage.
  • Matches are usually 20–30 minutes each, with a 5-minute halftime break.
  • Teams earn 3 points for a win, 1 point for a draw, and 0 points for a loss.

Semi-Finals

  • The top two teams from the group stage advance to the semi-finals.
  • Matches are more competitive, often decided by extra time or penalties if tied.
  • The semi-finals determine which two houses proceed to the grand final.

Finals

  • The best two teams face off in the Football Final Match.
  • Referees ensure fair play and adherence to rules.
  • The champion house lifts the trophy after the final whistle, followed by celebrations and photos.

Rugby Events

  • The two strongest teams meet in the final showdown.
  • Before kickoff, both teams line up for introductions and a short motivational chant or team huddle.
  • The final match is full of power, spirit, and crowd excitement,
    ending with the awarding of the Rugby Trophy to the winning house.

Special Awards

  • Best Player (MVP) – for consistent performance and leadership.
  • Best Tackler – for strong defensive play.
  • Top Try Scorer – for scoring the most tries
  • Most Disciplined Team – for teamwork and respect for rules.

Conclusion

The Football and Rugby events together form the backbone of the Sports Gala.
They promote discipline, teamwork, and healthy rivalry, creating memories
that define the school’s athletic spirit.
